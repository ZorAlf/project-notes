When it comes to impulse control, the mind is our definite test.

If we are not aware, tiny problems can easily open the window to a whole stream of negativity, fear, and self-doubt that our whole world is dark.

Here are a few tricks to resist those demons:

 1. Close the window as soon as it opens:

Whenever negative thoughts enter your mind, neglect them as soon as you become aware and counter them with a positive one. The longer you wait, the more dense that negativity becomes, it builds up and becomes uncontrollable. This is a difficult habit, but it becomes automatic with practice.

2. Thought Audit:

Review your thoughts critically, like a business would with finances. Assess which thoughts are assets (positive, uplifting, productive) and liabilities (negative, destructive, unproductive). This helps develop a more analytical approach to managing mental content, reducing emotional responses, and increasing logical handling.

3. Ecotherapy:

Engage with nature to reset your cognitive patterns. Studies show that spending time in natural environments can lead to significant improvements in mood and cognitive functioning. The unpredictability in perspectives, makes them seen as smaller and more manageable.
