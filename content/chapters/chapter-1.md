## Cortisol
What is that? Cortisol is a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal gland. It plays a crucial role in various functions including regulating metabolism reducing inflammation and controlling the sleep-wake cycle. Additionally, cortisol is involved in the body's response to stress helping to mobilize energy and cope with challenging situations.

## Hypothalamus
The hypothalamus is a structure deep within your brain. It's the main link between your endocrine system and your nervous system. Your hypothalamus keeps your body balanced in a stable state called homeostasis.

The hypothalamus is a crucial part of the brain that maintains the body's internal balance. It regulates body temperature, hunger and thirst, sleep-wake cycles, emotions, hormones, and reproductive function.

![Hyphothalamus](https://cdn.flintrehab.com/uploads/2020/03/hypothalamus-damage-1.png)

To keep your hypothalamus healthy you need to maintain a balanced diet exercise regularly get enough sleep manage stress and maintain a healthy weight remember to limit alcohol and illegal substances.

In stressful situations, the hypothalamus activates the HPA (hypothalamic pituitary adrenal) axis, leading to the release of cortisol. ## Melatonin Release
![Melatonin Release](https://www.physio-pedia.com/images/thumb/e/e8/Melatonin_production_in_24_hour_cycle.jpeg/436px-Melatonin_production_in_24_hour_cycle.jpeg)

Melatonin is a hormone produced by the pineal gland a tiny pine cone-
shaped gland located deep within the brain. It is often called the sleep hormone because its release is heavily influenced by dark and light.

**Darkness triggers melatonin production**

When darkness comes the pineal gland gets the signal to start producing
melatonin its levels continuously increase throughout the night reaching
a peak in the early hours of the morning and slowly decreasing as daylight comes. This natural rhythm helps regulate our sleep-wake cycle promoting sleepiness at night and alertness during the day.

**Light suppresses melatonin release**

Exposure to light particularly blue light emitted from electronic devices can suppress melatonin production which is why it is often recommended to avoid screens at least an hour before bedtime. Support healthy melatonin release:

 1. Practice good sleep hygiene: set a regular sleep schedule, create a relaxing bedtime routine, and avoid caffeine and alcohol before bed.
 
 2. Get regular sun exposure: spending time outdoors during the day can help regulate your circadian rhythm.
 
 4. Dim the lights at night: avoid bright lights at least an hour before bedtime especially blue light from screens

**Circadian Rhythms**
![Circadian Rhythms](https://www.news-medical.net/image-handler/picture/2021/2/shutterstock_1742387510.jpg)

Circadian Rhythms also known as circadian cycles are the natural timers in our bodies it follows a 24-hour cycle and regulates the various physical mental and behavioral changes we experience throughout the day and night. The main conductor of these rhythms is the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) located in our brain SCN receives signals from the environment mainly light in the darkness and regulates the release of hormones and chemicals that affect our Physiology and behavior.

Circadian rhythms operate in 24-hour cycles where processes in our body fluctuate and peak at certain times of the day.

## Adrenaline/Epinephrine
Adrenaline, or epinephrine, is a hormone and neurotransmitter produced by the adrenal glands at the top of each kidney. It's released into the bloodstream in response to stressful or dangerous situations, triggering the famous fight-or-flight response.

**What does adrenaline do?**
 - Increased heart rate and blood pressure
 - Dilated Pupil
 - suppress non-essential-functions

![Adrenaline](https://encrypted-tbn0.gstatic.com/images?q=tbn:ANd9GcT-EkQGEobdqWh39pUIqs6mBu7cs8KcRZPPGQ&s)

**Adrenaline = Cortisol**

Both adrenaline and cortisol are hormones that work together to prepare the body for action in stressful situations. Adrenaline, released rapidly from the adrenal glands, immediately increases heart rate, blood pressure, blood sugar levels, and pupil dilation, creating a burst of energy and focus. In contrast, cortisol is released in response to prolonged stress and has different effects. It also increases blood sugar levels but can suppress non-essential functions while boosting immune function. Cortisol helps maintain alertness during long exams and provides energy during extended crises.

## Adenosine
Adenosine is a key molecule found in every body cell with various important functions it helps regulate heart rate sleep and blood flow adenosine plays a crucial role in managing energy levels by controlling the production of ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate) in cells. When ATP levels decrease adenosine levels increase and the body slowdowns its activity to conserve energy in the brain acts as an inhibitory neurotransmitter reducing nerve activity and causing relaxation and drowsiness it also has an impact on heart rate typically slowing it down but it can also cause irregular heartbeats in some cases.
