*Suffering Makes You Beautiful*

— Friedrich Nietzsche

Suffering or pain in broad sense, may be an experience of unpleasantness or aversion, possibly associated with of harm or threat of harm in an individual. Suffering is often categorized as physical or mental.

Suffering occurs in the lives of sentient beings in numerous manners, often dramatically. As a result, many fields of human activity are concerned with some aspects of suffering.

There is theories of psychology present that humans sometimes seek out suffering. Many existentialists believe that suffering is necessary to find meaning in our lives. Existential positive psychology is a theory dedicated to exploring relationship between suffering and happiness and the belief that true authentic happiness can only come from experiencing pain and hardships.

## What makes man most healthy and spiritually alive?

That the greatness within man could only be manifested through great aim, self- overcoming, and from acquiring a taste for suffering and danger.

**1. Perspective on growth:** 

Suffering can open our eyes to the hardships faced by others, fostering empathy and compassion. It can also challenge our assumptions and push us outside our comfort zones, leading to personal growth and resilience.

**2. Finding purpose:**

Overcoming suffering can inspire a sense of purpose and drive individuals to help others who face similar struggles. This can lead to meaningful pursuits and contributions to the world.

**3. Appreciation for life:**

Experiencing hardship can make us appreciate good times and simple joys in life more deeply.

**4. Building strength and resilience:**

Facing and overcoming challenges can equip us with strength and resilience to navigate future difficulties.
