1. Concentrate on forming habits by taking small steps forward every day.
***
2. Just start working. Force yourself to start and to make the first step. The hardest step is always the first step. Once you take the first step you forget about procrastination and the job gets done.
***
3. Consider your energy levels. Take regular breaks throughout the day (walk, stretch, eat, water, nap, etc.) find your peak productive hours, and push yourself, when your energy is low take those breaks.
***
4. Overcome the fear of failure, the fear of failure can lead to procrastination. Although failure is a key component of success, you can't have one without the other. If you want to succeed faster, you have to fail more. Learn from those failures and thrive.
***
5. Don’t let perfectionism hold you back. Chasing after perfection can lead to procrastination, but understanding that nothing will ever be perfect empowers you to conquer this challenge. By breaking the cycle of perfectionism, you'll unlock incredible opportunities you never thought possible.
***
6. Creating a list can help you feel productive. Checking off items as "done" keeps you motivated and organized. Additionally, making a list decreases the chances of forgetting tasks.
