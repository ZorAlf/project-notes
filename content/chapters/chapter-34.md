**Humans Are Terrifying**

In the year 2145, Earth has turned into a wasteland, a silent, ruined dystopia brought about by centuries of human greed and destruction. The sky is forever gray, shrouded in a cloud of smog, and the forests that were once lush have withered into skeletal remains. The oceans, now toxic and lifeless, lap at shores littered with the debris of humanity’s golden age. The few remaining cities are fortresses of steel and concrete, towering monoliths serving as grim reminders of human ingenuity turned to evil. Between the ruins and decay, humanity reigns supreme, feared and reviled by every other living creature. The animal kingdom—those who have not gone extinct—has learned to avoid humans at all costs. Humans have become the ultimate predators in their relentless pursuit of progress.

Stories passed down from generation to generation among animals paint a bleak picture of human history. Large forests have been burned to the ground, rivers are polluted by industrial waste, and the sky has turned pitch-black due to endless wars. Humans hunt not for survival, but for entertainment, for trophies, and purely for pleasure. Birds, which once flew freely in the sky, now fly in erratic patterns to avoid the human drones patrolling the sky. The remaining mammals have become nocturnal, hiding in the shadows, constantly fleeing from human invasions.

The irony is inevitable. Humans, who were once the Earth’s caretakers and protectors, have become the most feared creatures on the planet. The creatures that once worshipped humans as gods now view them as bringers of death and destruction.

Deep within the heart of the last great city, the remnants of human civilization tell a story of lost ideals. Dusty old notebooks and weathered libraries hold tales of a time when people celebrated Earth Day, planted trees, cleaned up the oceans, and launched campaigns for wildlife conservation. These records reveal a world where humans lived in harmony with nature, where technological wonders coexisted with pristine wilderness.

But now all of that is just a story, a fragment of a forgotten era. People today, driven by despair and the instinct for survival, have lost their way. They have become the very monsters they once fought. The wolves—gaunt and starving—watch from afar, their eyes radiating fear and despair. They, too, are victims of this dystopian nightmare, just like the remaining humans. On rare occasions, humans show acts of kindness toward these creatures, tossing scraps of food their way. These small gestures are a glimmer of hope, a reminder that humanity still possesses the potential for compassion. Yet, the haunting truth remains: humans are terrifying. They have brought the world to its knees, and now, in their struggle to survive, they continue to spread fear and destruction. Humanity’s greatest enemy has always been itself.

In the twilight of civilization, as the remaining humans struggle to survive within their concrete fortresses, the Earth remembers. It remembered the days of harmony and balance, and it mourned what had been lost. Yet, in the hearts of a few, there remains a glimmer of hope that one day, humanity too will remember, and strive to become the guardians they were meant to be.
