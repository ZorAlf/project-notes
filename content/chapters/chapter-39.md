Memento Mori is an artistic or symbolic concept that serves as a reminder of the inevitability of death. This idea has its roots in the philosophies of classical antiquity and Christianity, and it became prominent in funerary art and architecture from the medieval period onward.

What happens after death? The one thing we know for sure is that death is inevitable. The uncertainties around the topic have led to the creation of different art forms which constantly remind us that our life on this earth along with all its delights irrevocably comes to a halt.


Death is often celebrated in art and they explain how each period of human history perceived death. Every culture has rituals surrounding death, appearing in artwork as icons and colors. Hourglasses and wilted flowers for the Dutch, the cuckoo bird in Japan, and the Totenkopf in Germany.

In the end, whether we like it or not, we do have to welcome it.

> Death is the only phase which one doesn't escape.
