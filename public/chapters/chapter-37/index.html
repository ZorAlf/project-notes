Chapter 37 — Types of Procrastinators

1. The Distracted Procrastinator#

Typical Behaviors:

  • You don’t respect your time blocks and allow yourself to be distracted.
  • Most of your day consists of minor tasks.
  • You don’t feel 100% present in your work.

Solutions:

  • Block out distractions in your environment.
  • Have a list of Not to-dos.
  • Plan for breaks in your study sessions, incorporate a reward system.

2. The Busy Procrastinator#

You’ve committed to too many projects to the point of having no time left.

Typical Behaviors:

  • You do a multitasking job.
  • You get things done off your to-do list but not seeing any results.
  • You hop from project to project at once.

Solutions:

  • Choose 3 most important tasks of the day, eliminate the rest. 80-20.
  • Rule-only use the best-performing ways to achieve objectives.
  • Prioritize your goals and learn to say no when it does not align.

3. The Perfectionist Procrastinator#

You want to improve the quality of your work but you have issues prioritizing the big things over the small details.

Typical behaviors:

  • You know what and how to do it but your thoughts pull you back.
  • You spend hours at the beginning.
  • You keep wanting to add one more change.

Solutions:

  • Shift your focus from results to actions until you get used to your routine.
  • Set realistic time limits for different sections of your works.
  • Keep your tasks bite-sized rather than an “all-or-nothing” mentality.

4. The Guilty Procrastinator#

Your interpretation of failure is preventing you from working past results are triggering negative thinking.

Typical behaviors:

  • You accept distractions thinking you’ve lost but think things will eventually work out.
  • You slack on healthy habits.
  • You feel people around you are mad or don’t understand your position.

Solutions:

  • Switch out self-criticism for self-compassion. Forgive yourself for procrastinating.
  • Realize that you did the best you could.
  • Do more of what you want to do opposed to what negative emotions urge us to do (meditate, exercise, and mindfulness).

5. The Disorganized Procrastinator#

You have poor time management/prioritizing skills. You hate routines and think they take away your freedom.

Typical Behaviors:

  • You waste time preparing your workspace when you want to work.
  • You do quick tasks and other small jobs that fill your day.
  • Even If you recognize you are procrastinating you would rather finish what you’re doing instead of the important work

Solutions:

  • Try prioritizing with the Eisenhower Matrix.
  • Schedule a few 60 minutes of power blocks in your day to work on your important work and time-block your days.

6. The Dreamer Procrastinator#

You overestimate your skill and underestimate the challenge. You delay because you are bored/overconfident with the task.

Typical Behaviors:

  • You don’t write down deadlines in your calendar.
  • You say ‘You’ll start tomorrow’ but repeat yourself until you find the deadline is the next day.

Solutions:

  • Increase your sense of urgency by committing to a deadline.
  • Stop talking and start doing.
