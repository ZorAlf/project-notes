Chapter 40 — The Last Man

FrAanx is the only conscious human in a world full of delusions. He woke up from a dark, boring, and empty dream, only to realize that the differences in his mind were merely tricks. All this time, FrAanx felt that his left side was just a shadow of his true reality. With this new awareness, FrAanx decided to escape from a place where other humans were still trapped in unconsciousness.

In his search for freedom and true happiness, FrAanx reveals his selfish side, as he believes that only he can understand this reality. He realizes that others will never be able to feel what he feels. However, FrAanx’s journey is not without obstacles. He must face an unknown world and humans who are still trapped in their own illusions. FrAanx struggles to find a place where he can truly live and experience the happiness he has always dreamed of.

FrAanx took his first steps into an unknown world, leaving behind the silent realm of dreamers. The air outside felt stifling with artificial warmth; the sky glowed an frightening purple, as if the world itself were resisting his awakening. He walked through deserted streets, passing people frozen in their routines, their eyes vacant, their movements stiff. They breathed, spoke, and existed, but none of them were alive.

At first, FrAanx felt a surge of triumph. He had freed himself. He alone possessed the clarity of mind to see the world as it truly was. But as the days passed, an unbearable silence enveloped him. Conversation was pointless; no one understood him. He tried to shake them, to shout at them, to shatter the illusions holding them down. They just stared at him without seeing, without hearing. As if he were a ghost haunting a world that had died.

Loneliness consumed his thoughts. It wasn’t just being alone—it was the realization that he would never again feel like he belonged anywhere. Every place he passed through felt colder than the one before. The city walls rise like prison bars, the sky stretches like a cruel void. He found no peace, no friends, only the endless echo of his own thoughts.

Then came the whispers….

At first, they were distant, almost imperceptible, like the wind rustling through dead leaves. But soon, they grew clearer, forming fragmented words, and sentences. Someone—or something—was calling to him. FrAanx followed the whispers through winding alleys and abandoned buildings until he reached the outskirts of the city, where the neon glow faded into darkness. There, he found her.

A woman, standing at the edge of the world, staring into the void. Her eyes were sharp, filled with an awareness that mirrored his own. She turned to him as if she had been expecting him all along. “You finally made it,” she said.

FrAanx hesitated. “Who are you?”

She smiled, but there was no warmth in it. “Like you, I woke up. And like you, I suffered. But I found something beyond the suffering.”

FrAanx’s heart was pounding. “What did you find?”

Her expression darkened. “The truth. And the truth is worse than the dream.”

Her expression was sad. “The truth. And the truth is worse than a nightmare.”

For the first time since waking up, fear crept into FrAanx’s chest. He had spent so long trying to escape, believing freedom would bring happiness. But now, staring at the woman who had walked this path before him, he realized something terrible:

Perhaps the dreamers were the lucky ones.
