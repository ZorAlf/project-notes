Chapter 33 — Humans Are Terrifying part 0

Humans Are Terrifying

Humans may not be the strongest, fastest, or biggest creatures, but what makes them truly terrifying is their endurance and persistence. Ancient humans used a hunting technique called persistence hunting, where they would chase their prey for hours until it collapsed from exhaustion. Unlike other animals, humans can run long distances without overheating because they sweat efficiently. Even when food is scarce, their bodies can survive by burning stored fat, allowing them to keep moving forward. This relentlessness is frightening, as many animals simply give up when they realize they cannot outrun a human.

Beyond hunting, humans have shown a terrifying ability to harm their kind. Throughout history, humans have committed some of the worst atrocities imaginable, from genocides to brutal wars. The greatest threats to humanity have not always been natural disasters or external enemies but rather humans themselves. Despite this, they continue to endure even in the face of catastrophe. Plagues like the Black Death wiped out massive portions of the population, yet humanity pushed through and rebuilt. Their ability to survive and adapt is nearly unmatched.

If aliens were to visit Earth, they would be shocked by how humans have taken control of the planet. These small creatures have not only survived but have also manipulated nature to create devastating weapons capable of mass destruction. Even their survival instincts make them nearly unstoppable humans can endure severe injuries, lose large amounts of blood, and still keep fighting. Their technological advancements have been incredibly rapid. Just 200 years ago, there were no railroads, and only 75 years ago, humans had not yet reached space. Now, artificial intelligence is advancing at an exponential rate, and space travel is becoming more common.

Humans do not merely destroy; they also explore and conquer. Their curiosity drives them to seek out and understand everything, even at great personal risk. They climb mountains, dive into the oceans, and venture into space—not because they have to, but simply because they want to. If aliens were hiding on Earth, humans would eventually find them—perhaps by accident—and study them out of deep curiosity. Their need to leave a mark on the world is undeniable, as they continue to build, document, and create for future generations.

Yet, despite all this power and intelligence, humans are ultimately alone in the vast universe. They exist on a tiny planet, uncertain of their purpose but determined to keep moving forward. Even in the face of the unknown, they do not stop. No matter how terrifying the universe may seem, it should fear humanity just as much. If history has proven anything, it is that humans will push forward relentlessly, leaving their mark on the cosmos as one of the most persistent and unstoppable forces in existence.
