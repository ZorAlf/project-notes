Chapter 10 — Pain of Regret and Pain of Discipline

Table of Contents
  • The inner sloth
  • Your dreams are your guidance
  • Regret comes last, and it lasts a while

Just do something, do anything!!!

Start with small things, don’t need to complete a goal or set milestones just yet.

Small things such as:

  • Wake up early and make up bed
  • Stop scrolling and put your phone away
  • Stretch and sunbath for at least 10 minutes a day
  • 5-10 pages per day

Once it’s become a daily routine, kick it up and add more habits on top of the current one. Find the momentum first and improve the habits later on.

The inner sloth#

“the inner sloth is a voice inside your head that wants you to stop moving and just lay around doom-scrolling”

The only way to beat your inner sloth is to see a bigger picture. Think about how different your life will be if you just do one thing, and then do the opposite of what it wants you to.

Your dreams are your guidance#

Whenever you feel lost, think about your dreams. Use them as a guide to remember why you are doing this in the first place. Your dreams will play an essential role in your growth. They guide you on the right path, otherwise you’d walk in circles without directions.

Regret comes last, and it lasts a while#

When you are disciplined, you are choosing to do so. It is a conscious effort, you have an option whether to eat healthily and sleep early or not because it is all in the present. But when you regret something because you were not disciplined, there is nothing you can do but accept the past. You can only suffer until you let go and move on. Regrets come at the end.

It is always your choice whether to be disciplined or give in to your desires, remember the pain of discipline tastes more bitter than the pain of regret.
