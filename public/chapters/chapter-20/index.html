Singularities#

A singularity is a point in spacetime where gravity becomes infinitely strong. According to physics, the center of a black hole is a singularity. Gravity is so strong that not even light can escape its pull.

Read More: https://www.livescience.com/what-is-singularity

Space and Time#

Our everyday experience of reality consists of three spatial dimensions (length, width, and height) and one temporal dimension (time) According to Einstein’s theory of relativity, space and time are not separate entities but rather interwoven into a single fabric called space-time. The curvature of spacetime is what we experience as gravity.

Read More: https://www.livescience.com/space-time.html

Dark Matter#

Dark matter is a mysterious substance that makes up about 27% of the universe’s mass. We can’t directly observe dark matter, but we know its gravitational effects on visible matter. For instance, galaxies spin too fast to be held together by the gravity of the stars and gas that we can see.

Read More: https://www.anl.gov/science-101/dark-matter-and-dark-energy

Dark Energy#

Dark energy is another mysterious component of the universe’s energy. Even stranger than dark matter. Dark energy seems to be causing the universe to expand at an accelerating rate.

Read More: https://www.space.com/dark-energy-what-is-it

The Observable Universe#

The observable universe is the part of the universe that we can see. It is limited by the speed of light, which means that we can only see things that have emitted light that has had time to reach us since the Big Bang. The diameter of the observable universe is estimated to be about 93 billion light years.

Read More: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Observable_universe
