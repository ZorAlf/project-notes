Chapter 32 — Why Solitude Promote Greatness

Why Solitude Promotes Greatness - The Benefits of Being Alone

The idea is that solitude is not the same as loneliness and can actually bring great benefits. While loneliness is harmful to health, solitude helps in self-development, personal freedom, and mental well-being. Many great thinkers and leaders, such as Jesus, Buddha, Nietzsche, and Thoreau, found inspiration and clarity through solitude. They believed that being alone allows a person to think freely, develop their character, and escape the pressures of society.

Solitude provides freedom that social interactions cannot offer. When people are around others, they often feel pressure to act a certain way or meet expectations. This can limit personal growth. Philosophers like Arthur Schopenhauer and Jean-Paul Sartre pointed out that being alone allows a person to be truly themselves without worrying about how others see them. In solitude, people can reflect deeply, understand their emotions, and connect with their inner selves.

Many historical figures, including Admiral Richard Byrd and May Sarton, experienced profound personal transformations during long periods of solitude. Byrd, who spent months alone in Antarctica, found that solitude changed his perspective on life. Similarly, the Japanese psychiatrist Shoma Morita developed a therapy method that required patients to spend time alone to confront their emotions and heal naturally.

Different cultures have recognized the value of solitude. For example, the Tarahumara Indians of Mexico encouraged children to spend time alone to build independence, and many ancient societies included solitary quests in their rites of passage into adulthood. However, modern society tends to avoid solitude. People often turn to screens and distractions to escape being alone, which might contribute to rising mental health issues. The philosopher Blaise Pascal once said that many of humanity’s problems come from people’s inability to sit alone in silence. This suggests that learning to embrace solitude could lead to a stronger and more balanced life.
